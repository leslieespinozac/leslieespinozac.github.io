About me

From the streets of Lima to the heart of Silicon Valley

I'm Leslie Anasu Espinoza Campomanes — biomedical & software engineer, and AI specialist in medicine. I was born on the outskirts of Lima, Peru, from Quechua-Chanka roots, an indigenous Andean culture I carry with immense pride.

I learned English in three years. I was admitted to over 12 of the world's top universities. I chose Stanford, where I completed both my undergraduate degree in Bioengineering and my Master's in Biomedical AI at Stanford Medicine — on a full scholarship.

"I am going to be the person in charge of my sister's future."

— Leslie, Stanford Magazine, 2020

Throughout my career I have collaborated with Nobel Prize winners, contributed to cancer and chronic disease research, published in journals like Current Biology, and served as a teaching assistant at Stanford. Today I am a Radiation Oncology Fellow at Stanford Medicine and work at Anatomage — the world leader in 3D medical technology.

Social impact

Science is also an act of service

I deeply believe the knowledge I have acquired must return to the communities that need it most. My work does not end in the lab or classroom — it lives in the streets, rural schools, and organizations fighting to change the system.

As Girl Up Ambassador for Peru at the UN, I worked to empower girls and young Latinas in access to education, health, and technology. I believe the next biomedical engineer can come from any Andean community in Peru, if given the opportunity.
